title: "What changes for you"
subtitle: "three lines"
bullets:
  - tag: "ship faster"
    heading: "Keep your stack"
    body: >
      The partials, controllers, and ActiveRecord queries you have today keep
      working. No DSL to learn, no component library to adopt, no SPA rewrite.
      New teammates are productive on day one.
  - tag: "move less code"
    heading: "Delete the plumbing"
    body_html: |
      Drop the <code>broadcasts_to</code> calls, the <code>after_update_commit</code> callbacks, the <code>touch:</code> chains, the cache-key book-keeping. Upkeep derives the dependency graph for you, so you stop maintaining it.
  - tag: "users feel it"
    heading: "Updates that just appear"
    body: >
      A change to one record updates exactly the UI that depends on it, in
      every connected browser, in milliseconds. Focus, cursor position, scroll,
      and form state all stay intact. It feels like magic.
